ITEM 1 — Linux "Copy Fail" (CVE-2026-31431) | CRITICAL | Actively Exploited

What happened: CISA added CVE-2026-31431 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, citing evidence of active exploitation. The vulnerability is a local privilege escalation (LPE) flaw that could allow an unprivileged local user to obtain root. The vulnerability affects virtually all Linux distributions running kernels released from 2017 until patched versions are applied, including Ubuntu 24.04 LTS, Amazon Linux 2023, Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL 10.1), and SUSE 16.

Why it matters: Successful exploitation leads to full root privilege escalation and could facilitate container breakout, multi-tenant compromise, and lateral movement within shared environments. Its reliability, stealth (in-memory-only modification), and cross-platform applicability make it particularly dangerous in cloud, CI/CD, and Kubernetes environments.

Who is affected: Anyone running unpatched Linux systems — especially cloud and containerized environments (AWS, Azure, GCP).

What Osman should learn or do: Study Linux privilege escalation techniques on TryHackMe (the "Linux PrivEsc" room). Learn what AF_ALG sockets are. Understand how CISA's KEV catalog works — this is a SOC interview topic. Fixes are available in Linux kernel versions 6.18.22, 6.19.12, and 7.0.
